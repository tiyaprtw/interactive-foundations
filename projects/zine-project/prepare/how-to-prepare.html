Don't Panic

If You're Home

Remember to Protect Your Head First.

Drop, cover and hold on. Move as little as possible.

If you are in bed, stay there, curl up and hold on. Protect your head with a pillow.

Stay Away From Windows and Fire Alarms.

Stay away from windows to avoid being injured by shattered glass.

Be aware that fire alarms and sprinkler systems frequently go off in buildings during an arthquake, even if there is no fire.

Keep All Your Loved One Safe.

Stay alert, stay together, move quickly and protect one another. Stay alive.

If You Hear A Tsunami Warning, Evacuate Immediately.

Strong earthquakes usually followed by tsunami, when you got a tsunami warning or you notice that the water suddenly withdrawing from the sea, locate the tsunami sign near you and evacuate immediately to higher place, do not return until further notice.

If It's Stop Shaking, Find a Way Out.

Stay until the shaking stops and you are sure it is safe to exit. If you must leave the building after the shaking stops, if you're inside a building, use stairs rather than an elevator in case there are aftershocks, power outages or other damage.

If You're Outside

Avoid Big Trees and High Buildings.

Find a clear spot and drop to the ground. Stay there until the shaking stops (away from buildings, power lines, trees,streetlights).

If You're Driving, Pull Over.

If you are in a vehicle, pull over to a clear location and stop. Avoid bridges, overpasses and power lines if possible.

Stay inside with your seatbelt fastened until the shaking stops. Then, drive carefully, avoiding bridges and ramps that may have been damaged.
